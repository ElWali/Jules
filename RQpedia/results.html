Machu Picchu

Peru, Cusco Region

An Incan citadel set high in the Andes Mountains in Peru, above the Urubamba River valley. Built in the 15th century, it's renowned for its sophisticated dry-stone walls that fuse huge blocks without the use of mortar, intriguing buildings that play on astronomical alignments and panoramic views.
